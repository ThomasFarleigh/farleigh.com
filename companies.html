Companies

Richard's philosophy was described by Internet Week: "When Farleigh invests in a firm, he checks the technology, the competition, customer endorsements and management teams, because 'there's more technology around than there is good management'".

The following list shows companies backed by Richard in their early stages and through their subsequent growth. An extremely exceptional proportion of these have achieved a stock market listing or been acquired.

Highlights include Home House, which after an extensive renovation to a Georgian mansion became one of London’s most fashionable private members’ clubs with him as the major backer and Chairman, Net-a-Porter, a now famous Haute Couture e-commerce site recently sold for $500m, Wolfson Microelectronics, a leading UK tech company providing parts for the iPod and X Box, where Richard underwrote some early fundraisings, Green Chemicals, with exciting new hair and fabric products, Innovative Physics with world leading radiation detection products, and Proximagen, a leading UK bio-tech specialising in neuro-science.

About

After a tough start in life, Richard Farleigh had a highly successful career in finance. He now operates as a business angel, and has backed the most start-ups in the UK. He appeared as a panelist on Dragons’ Den, and is the author of “Taming The Lion”.
